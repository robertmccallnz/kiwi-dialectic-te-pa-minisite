Street campaign generator · live

Build a poster for what's happening right now.

Pick a live indigenous-led campaign. Edit the slogan. Embed a QR code that links back to context. Download as SVG or PNG. Print A4 or A3 at home, the library, or any copy shop. Paste up legally. Every output respects the ethical protocols on this site — no AI-generated motifs, no language text published outside its community's consent, slogans sourced from real movements, not invented.

How this stays current. Thirteen curated categories are the spine — slogans, movement leaders, and source links come from public statements by the movements themselves. Once a week an automatic check reads RSS feeds from indigenous-led news outlets (ICT News, Cultural Survival, Waatea News, Survival International, Guardian First Nations) and routes new headlines into the matching category so what's on this page reflects what's on the streets.

      Detecting your region…

      Detection uses your browser's reported timezone, not your IP. If it's wrong, just ignore the hints — the universal advice still applies: cheapest is a public library or a Warehouse Stationery / Officeworks / Staples / FedEx Office self-serve copier.

      Know your rights. Postering and wheatpasting rules vary by city. As a baseline:
      • Aotearoa NZ: Most councils restrict postering to designated boards. Private property — owner's permission only. Community Law NZ.
      • Australia: Local council bylaws govern; "bill-posting" is generally an offence on public infrastructure. ALHR resources.
      • USA: First Amendment protects content; local "anti-posting" ordinances regulate placement. ACLU Know Your Rights.
      • UK/EU: Flyposting laws apply; check council/borough rules.
