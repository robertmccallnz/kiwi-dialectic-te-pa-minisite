# Facebook Page Setup — Te Pā Tūwatawata

A concise, opinionated setup for a Page optimised for **political activism, community organising, and rhizome-map engagement**. Total time: ~20 minutes.

---

## TL;DR

1. Create the Page as **Community** (not Business)
2. Name: **Te Pā Tūwatawata** · Username: `tepatuwatawata`
3. Bio: short, bilingual, link to course
4. Bury the personal profile link, surface the course
5. Pin a welcome post that introduces the rhizome map
6. Turn off comment filtering bots; turn on bilingual auto-replies
7. Post the 6 lead motifs as **native photo uploads** (not link cards)

Then watch the rhizome map light up.

## 1. Create the Page (2 min)

1. Open [facebook.com/pages/create](https://www.facebook.com/pages/create) on a laptop browser (the mobile flow hides options).
2. **Page name:** `Te Pā Tūwatawata`
   - The macrons matter. Facebook accepts them. If you ever rename later, you get 1 free rename then a 7-day cooldown.
3. **Category:** Pick **two** — Facebook lets you stack up to three. Recommended:
   - `Education` (primary — unlocks "Free Online Course" call-to-action button)
   - `Community Organisation` (secondary — signals civic intent to the algorithm)
   - Optional third: `Cause`
4. **Bio (255 chars max):** Use this draft:

   > Te Pā Tūwatawata — indigenous data sovereignty, working-class AI literacy. Free course, open kete. Mauri ora ki te tangata. → kiwidialectic.com

5. Hit **Create Page**.

**Why Community over Business:** Business Pages are pushed harder toward ad-spend prompts and treated as commercial in the algorithm. Community Pages get organic reach priority for civic content and qualify for Meta's Community Help / Crisis Response features later if you want them.

## 6. Privacy + safety settings (5 min) — critical for activism

These protect you, your readers, and the Page from coordinated takedowns.

### Page Roles
- **Only you** as Admin for now. If you add anyone later, give them **Editor** not Admin.
- Never accept role requests from accounts you don't personally know offline.

### Visibility
- **Page visibility:** Published (not Unpublished)
- **Country restrictions:** None — you want global reach for the multilingual launch
- **Age restrictions:** None

### Posts & Comments
- **Page Moderation:** Add a blocklist of slurs in EN/MI/SM/PT (Facebook has a default list — turn it ON, then add your own terms). Example terms to consider blocking: doxx-attempts, common racial slurs targeting Māori/Pasifika/indigenous Brazilian peoples.
- **Profanity Filter:** **Off**. (It blocks legitimate working-class voice and Te Reo terms it doesn't recognise.)
- **Comment Ranking:** **Off** — show comments chronologically. Ranking buries dissent that engages with your content, which is exactly what you want surfaced.
- **Tag Review:** On
- **Story Sharing:** On (you want shares)

### Messaging
- **Allow messages:** On — but set up an auto-reply (see §8)
- **Response assistant:** On for "instant reply" only; off for "away message" (away messages signal a slow Page to the algorithm)

### Notifications
- Turn on **all** notifications initially — you need to know within minutes if a coordinated reporting attack starts. After a month, dial down to your taste.

### Two-factor authentication (this is non-negotiable for political Pages)
- Personal account that owns the Page → Settings → Security → **Two-Factor Authentication** → use an authenticator app, not SMS.
- Add a **trusted contact** (someone offline who can vouch if you get locked out).
- Set up a **backup admin** within 30 days — ideally someone in a different country/timezone so a regional outage doesn't lock you out of your own Page.

## 7. Audience targeting (3 min)

Facebook killed the old "Preferred Audience" granular targeting in 2023, but the **Page Audience Insights** still work and the algorithm still uses your Page-level signals.

1. Settings → **Page audience preferences** (if available in your region) → set:
   - **Locations:** New Zealand, Australia, USA, UK, Canada, Brazil, Argentina, Paraguay, Sāmoa, Aotearoa diaspora communities
   - **Languages:** English, Māori, Sāmoan, Portuguese, Guaraní
   - **Interests:** indigenous rights, decolonisation, data sovereignty, free education, political philosophy, socialism, Pasifika culture
   - **Age:** 18–65+ (don't narrow — you want elders sharing too)

2. **Don't** set a "preferred audience" tighter than this. Tight preferred audiences = smaller initial reach pool = algorithm gets less signal = posts underperform = death spiral.

**Algorithm note (2025):** Meta announced in January 2025 they are now ranking civic/political content using **explicit signals** (likes, shares, follows) and **implicit signals** (dwell time) — the same way they rank everything else ([Meta Newsroom, Jan 2025](https://about.fb.com/news/2025/01/meta-more-speech-fewer-mistakes/)). This is good news: it means political content is no longer auto-suppressed. The implication is **your first 48 hours of engagement on each post determine its lifetime reach**. So you want a small group of supporters primed to like/comment within the first hour.

## 9. Pre-launch ritual — the first 6 followers

Before posting the 6 lead motifs, get the Page to **6 likes minimum**:

1. Invite yourself to like your own Page (use your personal profile)
2. Message 4–5 trusted comrades / collaborators — ask each to **like the Page AND turn on notifications** (the bell icon → "All Posts")
   - This is the single most important thing for the algorithm. 5 people with notifications-on gives you 5 guaranteed engagements in the first hour of every post, which signals to Facebook that the post is "performing" and triggers wider distribution.
3. Tell those 5 people which posts you're about to publish so they can engage **within the first hour** (not just liking — short comments outperform likes 5×).

Don't skip this. A Page with 0 likes posting 6 motifs gets buried. A Page with 5 notified supporters posting 6 motifs gets seeded into hundreds of feeds.

## 10. The 6 lead motifs — posting tactics

Once §1–9 are done, send me the Page URL and I'll fire all 6 via the connector. **But** the connector only supports text + link card. If you want maximum reach, post manually using these tactics:

### Native photo > link card
Native photo uploads get **~2.3× more organic reach** than link cards on Facebook in 2025 (Meta deliberately tunes for "things people stop and look at"). For each of the 6 posts:

1. **Download** the meme PNG from R2 (the URL in the launch pack)
2. Open Facebook → Page → **Photo/Video** → upload
3. **Paste the caption** from [LEAD-MOTIFS-LAUNCH.md](https://github.com/robertmccallnz/kiwi-dialectic-te-pa-minisite/blob/main/launch-mediakit/LEAD-MOTIFS-LAUNCH.md)
4. **Add alt-text** (the alt-text table is at the bottom of that file)
5. **Tag location:** Dunedin, NZ on posts 1–2; tag nothing on 3–6 (let the algorithm geo-distribute)

### Timing (NZST, your local time)
- Post 1 (Koru EN): **7–9am Tuesday** (US East Coast evening, UK afternoon, NZ morning — three audiences awake)
- Post 2 (Koru MI): **+30 min**
- Wait until **same evening 7–9pm NZST** (Brazil morning):
- Post 3 (Yvy Marã Ey PT): 7pm
- Post 4 (Yvy Marã Ey GN): +30 min
- Post 5 (Niho Mano SM): **+12 hrs** the next morning (Sāmoa awake)
- Post 6 (Niho Mano EN): +30 min

This staggered schedule means each post hits its primary linguistic audience during their waking hours, which is what triggers the algorithm to ride that engagement into wider distribution. Crucially, the **rhizome map will pulse three times in three regions over ~24 hours** — that's the visual proof-of-spread.

### After each post (within 60 minutes)
1. Reply to the first 2–3 comments yourself, in the post's language. Comments-from-Page-owner are a top algorithmic signal.
2. Share to your personal profile (don't @ tag friends — Facebook flags that as spam now; just share publicly).
3. Open the [rhizome map](https://kiwi-dialectic-te-pa-minisite.vercel.app/rhizome-mapper.html) and screenshot the new dot. Post the screenshot as a comment on the original post: "← live now on the map". This loops people through the map → course → back to FB.

## 11. Engagement with the rhizome map (this is the differentiator)

Most political Pages post and disappear. Yours has a **live visualisation** showing the reach in real time. Use it.

### Weekly cadence
- **Mondays:** Post a **map screenshot** showing the previous week's spread. Caption: *"Where the motifs went this week →"*
- **Wednesdays:** Post a new motif from the registry
- **Fridays:** Repost an older motif **with the map state visible** — proves longevity
- **Sundays:** A "kete" post — round-up of articles, quotes, links the community shared

### Engagement bait that's actually ethical
- Pin a comment on each motif post: *"Drop a 🌱 if this resonates"* — emoji reactions count as engagement and they're frictionless
- Ask language-specific questions in the language of the post: e.g. on the MI post, *"He aha ō whakaaro?"* — gets Te Reo speakers to comment in Te Reo, which Facebook then surfaces to other Te Reo speakers
- On the GN post, the question *"Mba'éichapa?"* — same dynamic for Guaraní speakers

### Cross-pollinate with the map
Whenever someone significant shares your post (an org page, an activist, a journalist), screenshot the **map dot that appeared from their region** and reply to them with it: *"Thanks for sharing — you just lit up a node in [region]"*. This is a unique experience nobody else on FB offers. It will get screenshotted onward.

## 12. Safety + resilience

Political Pages get reported. Build resilience in advance.

- **Back up your content weekly.** Settings → Your Information → Download Information → request a copy. Store it outside Meta.
- **Add a second admin within 30 days** — someone in a different country/timezone.
- **Never post anything that could be construed as direct incitement** (Meta's automated systems can't tell critique from incitement; they just delete).
- **Avoid:** specific named individuals targeted with abuse, calls to violence even rhetorical, copyrighted music in videos.
- **Embrace:** systemic critique, historical analogy, indigenous languages (Meta's automated moderation is far less aggressive in Te Reo/Sāmoan/Guaraní than in English — this is a quirk you can use ethically).
- If a post gets taken down: **don't repost it immediately** — that escalates. Appeal first ("Request Review"), and if denied, rewrite it lighter the second time and reframe.
