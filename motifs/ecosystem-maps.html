Pūnaha Hoahoa — System design

Te Pā Ecosystem Maps

How the Te Pā Tūwatawata ecosystem fits together — the marae-based relationships in Ōtepoti, the national network across Aotearoa, the free courses, the Systems Observatory that records activity, and the wider web of indigenous movements it connects to. Everything is woven through the Six Thinkers meta-layer. Every box is a live link — click a node to open its site.

Edge labels (flows): kōrero = dialogue · raraunga = data / sovereignty · kaihaukai = reciprocal exchange · mahika kai = provisioning · takarangi = spiral growth · ngaru = wave / propagation · whanaungatanga = kinship / solidarity.

Te Pā Ōtepoti

The local layer. Te Pā in Ōtepoti sits in relationship with mana whenua and community partners — each brings a different flow: data (raraunga), reciprocal exchange (kaihaukai), food-gathering knowledge (mahika kai), dialogue (kōrero) and capability-building (takarangi). Practice then propagates back out (ngaru).

Te Pā Aotearoa

The national layer. The Six Thinkers meta-layer sets the pedagogy; the Te Pā Aotearoa hub (te-pa.org) coordinates regional nōari and shares kōrero out to each. Each region feeds back a distinct flow — Ōtepoti returns raraunga, others return kaihaukai, ngaru and mahika kai.

Te Tirohanga Pūnaha — Systems Observatory

The feedback layer. The Observatory watches the whole ecosystem — it records activity across the courses, the regional nōari and partners, and returns raraunga (data / signals) to the Te Pā hub so the network can adapt. This closes the takarangi: observation feeds back into design.

Te Kōtuinga Whānui — Wider connections

The outward layer. Te Pā is not a closed system. Two mappers open it onto the wider world: the Rhizome Mapper links Te Pā to indigenous sovereignty, land-rights, data-sovereignty and environmental-justice movements worldwide (whanaungatanga); the Thinkers Mapper is a rhizome of the whole course catalogue that feeds the Six Thinkers layer. Together they connect the local kaupapa to a global network of struggle and solidarity. Click either mapper to explore.
