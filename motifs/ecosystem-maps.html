Pūnaha Hoahoa — System design

Te Pā Ecosystem Maps

How the Te Pā Tūwatawata ecosystem fits together — the marae-based relationships in Ōtepoti, the national network across Aotearoa, the free courses, and the Systems Observatory that records activity. Everything is woven through the Six Thinkers meta-layer. Every box is a live link — click a node to open its site.

Edge labels (flows): kōrero = dialogue · raraunga = data / sovereignty · kaihaukai = reciprocal exchange · mahika kai = provisioning · takarangi = spiral growth · ngaru = wave / propagation.

Te Pā Ōtepoti

The local layer. Te Pā in Ōtepoti sits in relationship with mana whenua and community partners — each brings a different flow: data (raraunga), reciprocal exchange (kaihaukai), food-gathering knowledge (mahika kai), dialogue (kōrero) and capability-building (takarangi). Practice then propagates back out (ngaru).

Te Pā Aotearoa

The national layer. The Six Thinkers meta-layer sets the pedagogy; the Te Pā Aotearoa hub (te-pa.org) coordinates regional nōari and shares kōrero out to each. Each region feeds back a distinct flow — Ōtepoti returns raraunga, others return kaihaukai, ngaru and mahika kai.

Te Tirohanga Pūnaha — Systems Observatory

The feedback layer. The Observatory watches the whole ecosystem — it records activity across the courses, the regional nōari and partners, and returns raraunga (data / signals) to the Te Pā hub so the network can adapt. This closes the takarangi: observation feeds back into design.
