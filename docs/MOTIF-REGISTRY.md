## 5. The map signal — what does it mean when a motif appears?

You asked: *one of ours is appearing on the map*. Here is the explicit chain.

When the **Koru meme PNG** you cited ([`meme_koru.png`](https://pub-bf8eea881c1e44d88eda5192c3b92291.r2.dev/motifs/memes/meme_koru.png)) shows up on the rhizome mapper, it means:

1. The map's **Node panel** has fetched `/motifs?region=maori` (or `?id=koru`) from the Cloudflare Worker, which returned the motif record including the R2 meme URL.
2. The mapper rendered the Koru node with its colour token (`#2ecc71`), opened the panel, and surfaced the meme PNG plus the four share buttons (X · FB · IG · Download).
3. The visit was logged into the **D1 analytics table** as a `motif_view` event with geo + referrer + module context. Subsequent clicks emit `share_x`, `share_fb`, `share_ig`, `download` events.
4. Because the meme is hosted on the **R2 public CDN**, every share carries the canonical Te Pā URL — no platform can break the chain back to the project.

**The map is now a tracker, not just a visualisation.** Every motif appearing there is proof that someone — somewhere — clicked through to the rhizome, opened the node, and engaged. The Koru is the first to spread because it was the launch motif and it sits at Module 1 (the entry-point module on the course map).

### Match motif → moment

Pick the motif that carries the day's political moment. Some pairings already wired into the database:

- **Koru** — language revival, education launch, Te Wiki o te Reo Māori, course enrolment windows.
- **Niho Taniwha** — corporate AI data-extraction stories, OIA-style exposés, surveillance reporting.
- **Pā Tūwatawata** — community-defence framings, digital-borders / data-localisation arguments.
- **Mangōpare** — strikes, ocean / fisheries sovereignty, sustained resistance.
- **Takarangi** — historic-cycle pieces, Waitangi Day, anniversaries of Crown breach.
- **Yvy Marã Ey** — Amazonian land defence, anti-mercantilisation argument in PT/GN.
- **Niho Mano** — refusal-to-ask-permission posts, Sāmoan diaspora politics.
- **Luta Pela Vida** — APIB mobilisations, anti-Marco-Temporal pieces, demarcação posts.

### Match language → audience

Always lead in the community's language; English is the parallel, not the primary. Hashtag stacks for each language are encoded in `social-kit/captions/campaign-captions-unified.json`. Suggested cadence:

- **Tue–Fri 9am–1pm** in the audience's local timezone (highest engagement window for political/educational content).
- **Anchor dates:** Aug 9 (UN Indigenous Peoples), Feb 6 (Waitangi), Apr 22 (Earth Day / Amazonia), Nov (APIB gathering), Oct 12 (Day of Indigenous Resistance / Brasil & Latin America).

### Run a campaign in five steps

1. **Pick the motif** that maps to today's political moment (see the theme index above).
2. **Grab the language variant** from `https://raw.githubusercontent.com/robertmccallnz/kiwi-dialectic-te-pa-minisite/main/social-kit/memes/meme_{motif}_{lang}.png`.
3. **Pull the matching caption** from `social-kit/captions/campaign-captions-unified.json` so hashtags and CTA URL are pre-wired.
4. **Post** with a single share button — the URL it carries (`?utm_source=tiktok&motif=koru`) feeds back into the analytics worker.
5. **Watch the map.** Every share, every download, every click puts another dot on the rhizome — that is the visible proof-of-reach you can show funders, partners, and the Trust board.

### Three things to do this week

1. **Launch the three lead motifs simultaneously** — Koru (EN+MI), Yvy Marã Ey (PT+GN), Niho Mano (SM+EN). One per language community on the same day. Cross-link in the captions.
2. **Cut the 1:1 square crops** for IG feed — script lives at `scripts/` in the repo, batch over all 30 memes with ImageMagick (`-gravity center -crop 1080x1080+0+0`).
3. **Animate the four highest-impact memes** into 4-second TikTok clips (motif unfurls in, text fades, watermark pulses). TikTok rewards motion over static.
